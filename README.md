# Spotify Business Intelligence Dashboard Project

## Project Overview

End-to-end business intelligence project analysing six years of Spotify financial and user data (Q1 2017 – Q1 2023), covering revenue streams, cost structure, user growth and subscriber monetisation across 25 quarterly periods and 18 variables.

## The Process

- Mapped business questions to 3 stakeholder profiles before touching the data — Head of Strategy, Director of Revenue and VP of Operations
- Examined 18 dataset columns to identify which metrics answered which questions and flagged 2 formula-based columns requiring recalculation in Tableau
- Designed an initial 3-dashboard plan, then consolidated to 2 dashboards after a stakeholder meeting, reassigning every element based on analytical logic
- Built 8 calculated fields to derive metrics not present in the source data
- Designed and assembled 2 interactive Tableau dashboards with 9 visualisations, 6 KPI tiles each and dynamic Year and Quarter filters
- Delivered 6 professional documents covering design plan, mockups, building guide, executive summary, presentation script and spoken walkthrough


## Dashboards

- **Dashboard 1 — Growth & Revenue Performance:** MAU growth line chart, Premium vs Ad revenue grouped bar, user composition area chart, revenue mix trend and ARPU trend line
- **Dashboard 2 — Cost Structure & Efficiency:** stacked cost breakdown bar, revenue vs gross profit dual line, cost efficiency ratio lines and gross profit comparison by stream


## Key Insights

- Premium ARPU declined 22% from its 2017 peak (€5.53 → €4.32), meaning revenue growth is driven by volume, not subscriber value
- The free-to-paid conversion rate has stayed flat at ~40% for six consecutive years despite nearly quadrupling the total user base
- Ad-supported gross profit was negative in 5 separate quarters — the free tier periodically costs more to run than it generates in advertising revenue
- Cost of Revenue efficiency improved from 88% to 75% of total revenue — the strongest operational gain in the dataset
- R&D is the only cost category growing faster than revenue, rising from 8.9% to 14.3% over the period
